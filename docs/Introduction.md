# The Troubleshooting Bible
### A portable, offline-first reference for IT professionals and support teams.

The Troubleshooting Bible is a self-contained knowledge base built for the field — not the office. Whether you're diagnosing a failing system in a server room with no internet, supporting a user on-site, or onboarding a new technician at 2 AM, this guide is designed to be exactly where you need it: on a USB drive, on your phone, in your browser, ready to go.

Every entry is written with one goal in mind — get you from problem to resolution faster, with less guesswork and fewer context switches.
---
## What's Inside
The Bible is organized into three entry types:

 - Vocabulary — Quick, plain-English definitions for technical terms. When you need to know what something is without wading through a textbook.
 - Encyclopedia Entries — Deep-dive reference articles on specific technologies, threat types, system behaviors, and diagnostic concepts. Understand not just the what, but the why.
 - How-To Guides — Step-by-step procedures for real tasks: configuration, remediation, triage, and more. Follow the steps, solve the problem, move on.

## How to Use It
Open index.html in any modern browser. No server required, no internet connection needed, no installation. Use the built-in search to find entries by keyword, or browse by category from the index.
The Bible is designed to live on a USB drive, an SD card, a phone, or a local folder. Take it with you. Share it freely. It works wherever a browser works.

## A Living Document
This is not a finished product — it's a growing reference. Entries are added as new problems are encountered, new procedures are standardized, and new knowledge is worth preserving. If you solve something the hard way and figure out the right way, it belongs here.
