Drastically reduces risk of credential or brute-force attacks. Even if the password becomes leaked, the attacker still needs the second factor. Many real-world security breaches were preventable if MFA had been enabled. Requires at least 2 of the following factors:

1. Something you know (password, PIN)
2. Something you have (smartphone, token)
3. Something you are (biometric: face, fingerprint, voice)

    *CompTIA A+*
