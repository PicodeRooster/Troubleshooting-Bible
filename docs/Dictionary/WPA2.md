Wi-Fi Protected Access 2 (WPA2) became standard after the fall of WEP and it is still widely used today. It employs AES encryption and supports two operational modes: Personal (using pre-shared keys) and Enterprise (using a RADIUS server and 802.1X authentication). Not immune to vulnerabilities.
