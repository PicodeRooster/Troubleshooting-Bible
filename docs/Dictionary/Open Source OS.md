**Open-source operating systems** provide access to the source code, allowing users to inspect, modify, and redistribute the software. This promotes transparency and community-driven development, and often comes at a lower cost. They are especially prevalent in academic, scientific, and server environments where customization and stability are critical.

	  *Examples: Linux, FreeBSD*
