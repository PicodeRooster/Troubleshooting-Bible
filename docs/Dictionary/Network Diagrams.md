Network documentation representing visual maps of complex infrastructure. A network diagram typically captures device names and roles, IP addressing schemes, subnetting, VLANs, routing paths, firewalls, load balancers, and cloud links. They demonstrate connections, map dependencies, and reveal bottlenecks before, during, and after implementation. They are essential when onboarding new technicians, responding to outages, or communicating with non-technical stakeholders.

**Best practice:** Maintain a master copy and a working version. Update diagrams after every hardware or topology change.
