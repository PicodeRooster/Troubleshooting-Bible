**Role-Based Access Control** (RBCA) is a principle that follows the procedure of assigning access to users based on roles within the organization. These roles are predefined, consistent and easily assigned. RBCA improves efficiency and security while ensuring users receive the correct permissions automatically according to their roles. Additionally, it allows for smooth transitions between career events, like promotions or departures, making the tasks more manageable within a large userbase. RBCA is strategic method that additionally supports compliance frameworks by documenting who has access to what and why. However, periodic audition is required to keep this model effective, making sure users do not accumulate roles they no longer need.
