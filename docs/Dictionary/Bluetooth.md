A short-range wireless standard operating on the 2.4 GHz ISM band, used to connect devices over a personal area network (PAN). Uses Frequency-Hopping Spread Spectrum (FHSS) to reduce interference. Two radio types: Classic (BR/EDR) for audio streaming and data transfer, and Low Energy (LE) for low-power IoT and positioning applications. Vulnerable to close-range attacks including Bluejacking, Bluesnarfing, and Bluebugging — especially in public environments.
