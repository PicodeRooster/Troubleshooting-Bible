A **lock screen** is a standard security feature of modern operating systems that prompts for credentials after a session times out, preventing unauthorized access to an unattended device.

Individual users can enable and configure the lock screen manually through their OS settings — including the option to disable password protection, though this is strongly **not** recommended.

System administrators can enforce lock screen policies and automatic timeout intervals, recommended: 5 to 10 minutes) across enterprise devices through Windows Group Policy or an MDM platform.
